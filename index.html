Introduction

The PD RTH Range+ is a framework designed to visualize and track previous day's Regular Trading Hours (RTH) ranges for futures markets. It identifies key price levels established during standard US trading hours (9:30 AM – 4:15 PM ET) and Globex hours, helping traders identify important levels and fair value zones.

This framework is built around IF-THEN sequence logic — backed by historical NQ data — to generate your daily directional bias.

📊
Range Functions & Plot Modes

The tool operates with two modes: "RTH" Range and "Previous Day" Range. These help you visualize the previous day's points of interest and swing high/low — framing the current day's candle in relation to both the RTH range and the full previous day range.

🎯
Projections Feature

The projection feature has two plot modes: "Fixed" and "Custom". The Fixed mode uses the Rule of Thirds with two key zones:

0.33 – 0.66
First deviation zone. Price often stalls and retraces back into range from here.
1.33 – 1.66
Extended deviation zone. Price exhausts above/below range at these levels.
⚡ Actionable Areas
These projection zones are where you expect price to lose momentum and retrace back into the range — your entry triggers for retracements and reversals.
⚖
Dynamic Premium & Discount

Dynamically identifies fair value and equilibrium at current price — updating until projections or levels are met and a swing high/low has formed.

Trading Range Sequences

The framework generates your daily bias by following IF-THEN sequences. These are data-backed by statistics and form the core logic of the Decision Engine above.

EQ → High EQ hit first → High is next probable target (~52–57%)
EQ → Low EQ hit first → Low is next probable target (~75%)
High → EQ PD High tagged → EQ is next target (~89–91%). Strongest mean reversion setup.
Low → EQ PD Low tagged → EQ is next target (~89–94%). Highest edge in the dataset.
→ Projection EQ → Fixed Projections (0.33–0.66 / 1.33–1.66) or Projections → EQ reversal
↩
Retracements vs Reversals
📉 RETRACEMENTS
Taken from projection zones back to Equilibrium. Participate in the move to fair value from the deviation of the range.
🔄 REVERSALS
Taken from projection zones all the way to opposing range levels — EQ or High/Low.
🕯
ETH Candle Transparency

Electronic Trading Hours (ETH) candles outside of 9:30 AM – 4:15 PM ET are displayed with reduced opacity on the indicator, helping you focus on RTH price action — which is the core basis for the framework's trading day.

◼ 4:15 PM — RTH Close ◼ 9:30 AM — RTH Open Outside these = ETH (lower opacity)

Overview

Defaults to the critical 6:00 AM – 9:00 AM ET window — a period often pivotal for setting the day's momentum before RTH commences.

The 1.33–1.66 projection area derives from a mathematical calculation called the "Rule of Thirds" — where algorithms in funds set logical profit-taking and position closing, making price behave in the sense of retracing or reverting from said levels.
Two Trade Scenarios · How to Use the Framework
Reversal / Fade
Fade the range breakout and capitalize on breakout failure, targeting opposing levels of the breakout direction.
→ Use the 1.33–1.66 zone to anticipate the reaction — whether a retracement or full reversal, it provides the move needed to capitalize.
Breakout / Continuation
Anticipate early breakouts using inner-range levels — Range Open or midpoint — as guidance for positioning.
→ Range Open becomes the target after a range break when remaining un-tapped. Statistically, retracement to range open post-sweep has a very high hit %.

FAQ

Am I allowed to add into a winning position larger than 5 micro contracts?

Yes, but manage the position properly and ensure that if you lose, your losing position is no larger than 5 micro contracts. Stay compliant with the RM loss limit. Break-even management after scaling is recommended.

I ended at a loss today that went $25 over the 15% DLL rule. Do I fail the program?

Small slippage overage may be reviewed with discretion. The 15% DLL is a hard rule for discipline, so plan for volatility and slippage. Example: with a $2,000 drawdown, daily loss should not exceed $300.

How does the daily loss limit work?

Daily loss limit is 15% of the account's maximum drawdown. If the account has a $2,000 loss limit, daily loss cap for the program is $300.

How do I claim the new account after losing an account while following the RM guarantee?

Open a support ticket and submit your account metrics so code usage and rule compliance can be verified. Once validated, a new account of the same size and firm can be issued.
